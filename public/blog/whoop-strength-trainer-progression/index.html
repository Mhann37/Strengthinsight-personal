How to See WHOOP Strength Trainer Progression (and Track Improvements Over Time)

WHOOP Strength Trainer is great for logging lifting sessions, but long-term progression isn’t always obvious. Here’s how to track it clearly.

What WHOOP Strength Trainer shows today

  • A record of your workouts (exercises, sets, reps, weight where available)
  • Session-level summaries (what you did in that workout)
  • Useful context alongside recovery and strain

The missing piece: “progression over time”

Progression usually means: are lifts increasing, is weekly volume trending up, and are muscle groups staying balanced over weeks and months.

The easiest way to track progression with WHOOP data

1) Track weekly volume

Weekly volume = Σ (reps × weight) across all sets in that week. Watch the 4–8 week trend, not daily noise.

2) Track top sets for 3–5 key lifts

Pick a small set of core movements and track your best set each week. Look for higher load at the same reps or more reps at the same load.

Why progression can look “flat”

  • Missing tracking data (weights/reps)
  • Too much exercise variation
  • No weekly baseline comparisons
  • Recovery/fatigue masking performance
