Can You Access Your WHOOP Strength Trainer Data? (2026 Answer)

By James · Founder, StrengthInsight · January 2026

Short answer: WHOOP's API exposes recovery scores, HRV, and sleep — but not your sets, reps, or weights from Strength Trainer. That data exists inside WHOOP, but they don't share it. Here's what you can access, what you can't, and the screenshot-based workaround that fills the gap.

What "access" means (and what most people want)

Most users aren't looking for raw sensor streams — they're looking for practical strength information:

  • Your workout history (dates, exercises, sets, reps, weight)
  • Trends like weekly volume and muscle-group balance
  • A way to review progression outside the session-by-session view

What the WHOOP API Actually Exposes

WHOOP has a developer API. Here's what it does and doesn't expose:

Available via API:

  • Recovery score (0–100)
  • HRV (heart rate variability)
  • Resting heart rate
  • Sleep performance and duration
  • Day strain score
  • Workout strain (effort score only — not exercise detail)

Not available via API:

  • Exercise names from Strength Trainer
  • Sets, reps, or weights logged
  • Individual lift history

WHOOP has this data internally. They don't expose it through the API. This has been an open request in the WHOOP developer community since Strength Trainer launched, with no official response from WHOOP as of early 2026.

What WHOOP provides today

WHOOP gives you excellent in-app visibility of individual Strength Trainer sessions. Accessing that data outside the app is where things become less straightforward.

  • In-app history: You can view past strength sessions and exercise details.
  • Exports / sharing: Export options vary and may not include every strength-specific field in a clean format.
  • Programmatic access: Availability depends on official APIs and what endpoints/fields are exposed — and as noted above, strength session detail is not exposed.

The Screenshot Workaround

Upload it to StrengthInsight — AI reads the sets, reps, and weights automatically and adds them to your lift history. No API access needed.

Practical ways to get Strength Trainer data out

1) Manual capture (fastest for progression)

If your goal is simply to see progression trends, the fastest "export" is often just capturing the workout summary details you already see — enough to compute weekly volume and trends.

  • Pros: quick, no setup, works for everyone
  • Cons: not a full historical export unless you do many sessions

2) Official export options (good for general history)

WHOOP may provide account-level export options for certain datasets. These can be useful for broader analysis, but strength session data can be harder to interpret depending on format and included fields.

  • Pros: more comprehensive than manual capture
  • Cons: may not be strength-optimised; fields can be missing or difficult to map

3) API access (best when available)

If WHOOP exposes strength-session fields via official APIs, programmatic access is the cleanest long-term route. The tradeoff is complexity: auth, permissions, field mapping, and keeping up with platform changes.

  • Pros: most scalable and automatable
  • Cons: requires developer effort and depends on what WHOOP actually exposes — currently not strength detail

What to do if your real goal is progression

If what you want is "am I improving?", you don't need perfect data export to start. A small slice of recent workouts is enough to generate useful trend views like:

  • Weekly volume (reps × weight)
  • Muscle group balance (chest/back/legs etc.)
  • Consistency (how regularly you're training each group)

FAQ

Can I export WHOOP Strength Trainer workouts to a spreadsheet?

Not directly. WHOOP doesn't offer a CSV export for Strength Trainer sessions. The closest option is exporting via screenshots and using a tool like StrengthInsight to extract and store the data in a queryable format.

Is there a simple way to see progression without exporting everything?

Yes. Start with a small set of recent workouts and track weekly volume and key lift top sets. You can build a useful trend picture without needing a complete historical export.

Will WHOOP add more strength data access over time?

Possibly. Platforms evolve — but relying on "future features" is frustrating if you want progression visibility today. Start with the data you can access now and refine later.

Will WHOOP ever expose Strength Trainer data via API?

As of early 2026, WHOOP hasn't announced any plans to expose Strength Trainer sets/reps/weights via the developer API. The request exists in the community but has had no official response.

Does Hevy have the same API limitation?

No — Hevy has a more open API that exposes workout history including exercises, sets, reps, and weights. If API access to your strength data matters to you, Hevy is the more open option. StrengthInsight supports both.
